Discrete Structure

Overview

I studied mathematical induction, logical proof, propositional and predicate calculus, combinatorics and discrete mathematics, basic probability theory, basic number theory: sets, functions, and relations, graphs, and finite-state machines. Going further, I also explored the applications to many areas of computer science, such as the RSA cryptosystem and web searching.

Networks

Overview

I studied different scientific perspectives to understanding networks and behavior. Drawing on ideas from economics, sociology, computing and information science, and applied mathematics, I focused on the emerging field of study that is growing at the interface of all these areas, addressing fundamental questions about how the social, economic, and technological worlds are connected. Thus, I practiced building mathematical models of the processes that take place in networks.

Personal Notes

(Click the image to see more)

Topics covered in the note: Graph Theory and Social Networks, Game Theory, and Markets and Strategic Interaction on Networks. I practiced social network analysis, including the famous 'strength of weak ties' hypothesis in sociology. I studied game theory through the context of examples, including the design of auctions and some 'paradoxical' phenomena surrounding network traffic congestion. Also, I studied the interactions among participants in a market and the network structure that provides a natural illustration of how graph theory and game theory can come together in the development of models for network behavior.

Topics covered in the note: Information Networks and the World-Wide Web, Network Dynamics: Structural Models, Institutions and Aggregate Behavior. I practiced modeling the Web as a network; focusing on how search engines make use of link information for ranking, how they use ideas related to power and centrality in social networks, and how they have implemented network-based matching markets for sellling advertising. I studied the exploration of how things flow through networks, how both behaviors and diseases can spread through a social network, and also some of the network phenomena that underpin the "six degrees of separation" effect.
